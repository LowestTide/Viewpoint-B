Viewpoint B: Louis Riel's trial was stacked against him and the verdict was politically motivated.

This article explores the controversial trial of Louis Riel, a key figure in Canadian history.

Louis Riel's trial in July of 1885 is widely regarded as one of the most famous trials evers conducted within Canada's history. However, this is primarily true, due to the multifarious ways that the trial and the verdict was politically altered and modified, all to procure illegitimate results, and to guarantee the ill-punishment of one Canada's most well-known patriots.

It is evident that the trial, and its eventual outcome was engineered in a way to exhibit the greatest possible punishment on Riel, when we consider that the trial was purposefully staged in the presence of an English-majority jury in Regina, as well as the fact that these people most definitely held biased and stereotypical beliefs against Riel. Riel was also charged with high treason, which was a charge that could be used to inflict the death penalty on Riel, due to an old English law dating back to 1342. This legal loophole was utilized, because none of the newborn Canadian laws at the time held the death penalty. This once again builds on the notion that Riel's trial was politically motivated. Additionally, Riel was seen to showcase several cases of mental instability, and insanity, considering that he'd been locked into an insane asylum for two years in March of 1876 to January of 1878 previously. This was consistent with his supposed ramblings of being the "prophet of a New World", which suggests that he may have been clinically insane, or at least mentally unstable during the rebellions that he conducted. Despite this, the appeals that had been sent to the Court of Queen's Bench of Manitoba, and the Judicial Committee of the Privy Council refused to process and administer the appeals. Furthermore, the prime judge of the case, Judge Richardson also refused to acknowledge any pleas, and/or appeals regarding his unkempt mental state, and the potential state of insanity that Riel may have been in.
